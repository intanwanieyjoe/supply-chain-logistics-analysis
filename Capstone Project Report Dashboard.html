Identifying Bottlenecks

This analysis reveals that PORT04 handles 97% of total shipment volume (4,754 out of 4,899 records) while simultaneously exhibiting the highest average shipping delays across all origin ports.

PORT09 shows moderate delays with 144 records (3% volume), warranting monitoring but lower priority intervention. PORT05 has minimal volume (1 record) and correspondingly low delays, confirming it's not a capacity constraint.

The extreme volume-delay combination at PORT04 validates the urgent need for capacity rebalancing, route diversification to PORT02 alternatives, and immediate operational review to alleviate network congestion and improve overall delivery performance.

Port Performance Analysis

Ports show varying efficiency levels across the Malaysian logistics network. This analysis compares average Total Lead Time against Transit Processing Time (TPT) for major origin ports. PORT05 demonstrates the worst performance with lead times significantly higher than TPT, indicating severe handling bottlenecks despite low volume. Conversely, PORT04 offers better efficiency with minimal gap between processing and total delivery time.

Freight Rate Anomalies

Analyzing the relationship between shipment weight and freight cost reveals significant pricing inefficiencies. While a linear correlation is expected, the data shows clusters of high-cost, low-weight outliers.

These outliers represent "Rush Orders" sent via air freight or expedited ground services. Reducing these ad-hoc emergency shipments through better inventory planning could save an estimated a meaningful reduction in annual logistics expenditure.

Carrier Performance: TPT vs On-Time Delivery

Dual-axis analysis showing carrier efficiency. V444_1 leads with the lowest TPT and highest on-time rate. Filter by carrier to highlight specific performance.

V444_1 stands out as the top-performing carrier, with the lowest Average TPT and the highest on-time delivery rate. This indicates fast and reliable service. In contrast, V444_0 is the dominant carrier by volume with moderate TPT.

V44_3 operates as Customer-Arranged Shipping (CRF), representing zero-cost freight for the firm. It shows the highest TPT variance and most inconsistent delivery performance among the three carriers, and should be excluded from standard carrier cost comparisons.

Port Impact: Shipment Volume vs. Average Lead Time

PORT04 dominates shipment volume with 4,754 shipments yet maintains the lowest average lead time at 3.0 days demonstrating strong operational efficiency at scale.

PORT09 processes 144 shipments with an average lead time of 3.75 days. It serves as the network's secondary hub and the most viable candidate for capacity expansion, with lead times only 0.75 days longer than PORT04.

PORT05 handles just 1 shipment with the slowest lead time of 5.0 days — 67% slower than PORT04. Its poor performance is likely due to limited carrier availability and lack of optimized scheduling from extremely low volume.

The extreme volume-delay combination at PORT04 validates the urgent need for capacity rebalancing, route diversification to PORT02 alternatives, and immediate operational review to alleviate network congestion and improve overall delivery performance.

Strategic Recommendations

⤮

Route Diversification

Re-route all non-urgent, standard-priority shipments from PORT04 to PORT02, starting with shipments that have lead time flexibility of 3 or more days. Keep PORT04 exclusively for time-sensitive and express-tier orders. Estimated reduction of 3.5 days in average lead time for re-routed shipments.

🤝

Carrier Consolidation

Renegotiate the logistics contract with V444_1 to consolidate a larger share of total shipment volume under a single preferred-carrier agreement. Incorporate tiered volume pricing and SLA clauses with formal penalties for failing to meet agreed delivery targets.

📈

Inventory Positioning

Raise safety stock levels at all warehouses served by PLANT12 to absorb normal demand variation without triggering emergency dispatch. Set minimum reorder point based on PORT04 average lead time plus a two-day buffer. Projected to reduce annual logistics costs by approximately 15%.

ANOVA: Transit Time Variance by Carrier

Statistical Proof (p < 0.05): One-Way ANOVA confirms carrier choice significantly impacts transit processing time (F = 754.0, p = 3.46e‑286). Each box shows median, IQR spread, and outliers per carrier.
❌ H₀ REJECTED ANOVA — One-Way Test

Null Hypothesis (H₀): All carriers have the same average TPT.

Conclusion: F = 754.0, p = 3.46e-286 < 0.05 — Carrier choice significantly impacts transit processing time.

Box = IQR (Q1–Q3) Whiskers = min/max Line = median

T-Test: PLANT09 vs Rest of Network

Statistical proof (p < 0.05): Independent T-Test confirms PLANT09 has a significantly higher average delivery delay than the rest of the network (T = 8.04, p = 3.03e-06). Error bars show 95% confidence interval.
❌ H₀ REJECTED T-Test — Independent (Welch's)

Null Hypothesis (H₀): Plant 09 Delay == Rest of Network Delay.

Conclusion: T = 8.04, p = 3.03e-06 < 0.05 — PLANT09 delays are significantly higher than the network baseline.

PDPA & Data Ethics Compliance Notice

This study was conducted in strict compliance with Malaysia's Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA). The dataset used is publicly available via Kaggle and contains no personally identifiable information (PII), no individual customer records, and no sensitive commercial data attributable to any identifiable legal entity. All shipment identifiers, carrier codes, port references, and plant codes within the dataset are anonymised numeric or alphanumeric labels that do not correspond to real-world organisations or individuals. No data collection, storage, or processing activity in this study involved the personal data of any natural person. The 3R principles — Reduce, Reuse, and Recycle of personal data — are fully observed; only the minimum data fields necessary to fulfil the stated analytical objectives were retained. Researchers are advised to maintain these standards if extending or replicating this analysis with live operational data.
